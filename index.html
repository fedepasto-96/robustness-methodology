The one-paragraph version

A TPI (a committee of indicators voting one direction) earns a deployment label from two independent questions. Robustness asks whether a real edge survives being perturbed — the same edge shows up at the tuned point, out-of-sample, across a parameter sweep, and across time folds. Stability asks whether the behaviour holds up under those same perturbations, even if the edge is modest. The key asymmetry: robust ⇒ stable, never the reverse. So the verdict is a ladder — ROBUST (edge that persists and is stable), BORDERLINE (stable, but the edge is incomplete: decaying, recent-only, or unverifiable), FRAGILE (fails any stability gate, or has no edge anywhere). The optimizer's job is to strive for a ROBUST 5-indicator TPI, fall back to STABLE, then reduce complexity — and only as a last resort (no ROBUST/BORDERLINE found at any size) deploy a FRAGILE that is still stable, has a real edge, and fails at most one other gate. An unstable FRAGILE, or one failing ≥2 gates, or one with no edge, is never deployed (deployability policy, 2026-07; ship_ok).

This document is the source of truth for the theory. The live verdict is computed by fep_robustness.fep_verdict(); the optimizer by tpi_autogen. Both are reconciled to what you read here.

0Part 0 — The system in plain language

Before the mathematics: what this machine is, why its headline numbers kept falling during July 2026, and why that was the goal. Everything below is restated precisely in the sections that follow; this part exists so the whole document can be understood without reading a single formula.

The system trades two sleeves — a crypto rotation and a stock rotation. For every pair of assets, a small committee of trend indicators (a Trend Probability Indicator, TPI) votes on which asset is currently stronger; a tournament adds the votes up and the portfolio holds the winners, or steps aside into cash or gold when the market-wide switches (the macro gates) read risk-off. Two different kinds of claims live in that machine, and the July 2026 campaign was about refusing to let one borrow credibility from the other: the claim that a single committee is trustworthy, and the claim that the whole rotation makes money.

Two questions, asked separately: does it make money, and is it dependable

EDGE — does it make money?

Four absolute floors on the risk-adjusted scorecard — Sharpe ≥ 0.7, Sortino ≥ 1.0, Profit Factor ≥ 1.5, Omega ≥ 1.15 — and clearing at least 3 of the 4 counts as a real edge. The same bar is applied three ways: on the full history, on the recent held-out window (out-of-sample, OOS), and on the worst row of the parameter sweep (nudge every knob ±3 and re-score — the edge must survive its worst neighbour).

STABILITY — is it dependable?

Six hard gates, each a separate axis, and failing any one is fatal (never averaged): survives re-tuning (walk-forward efficiency, WFE, ≥ 0.50, only enforceable with ≥ 30 out-of-sample trades); a calm parameter neighbourhood (worst-metric coefficient of variation, CoV, ≤ 0.20); makes its money in every stretch of history, not one lucky era (time-consistency: worst-metric CoV across 4 history slices ≤ 1.10, no dead slice); survives path shuffles (Monte-Carlo, MC, survival ≥ 0.50); the committee actually agrees (sign coherence ≥ 0.45); and a sane trade rhythm (turnover coherence ≥ 0.40) — plus validity checks on trade count and, for baskets, cross-asset breadth.

The two axes compose one way only: a real edge that is also dependable earns ROBUST; dependable but with incomplete evidence earns BORDERLINE; failing any dependability gate, or having no edge at all, is FRAGILE. Since July the same gates also act upstream: a candidate that whipsaws or scatters is rejected during generation, not politely labelled and shipped (§15).

The measurement math — what a “good” number is

Every verdict starts from four risk-adjusted ratios. Here is exactly how each is computed in backtester.py (including the non-textbook choices), made visual.

The Sharpe ratio — daily edge, blown up by √P

Mean excess return per unit of total volatility, then scaled by the square root of time.

$$ \text{Sharpe} = \sqrt{P}\;\frac{\overline{(r - r_f/P)}}{\sigma_{\text{pop}}(r - r_f/P)} $$
$$ P = \begin{cases} 365 & \text{crypto (default)} \\ 252 & \text{stocks} \end{cases}\qquad \sigma_{\text{pop}}=\sqrt{\tfrac{1}{N}\textstyle\sum(x_i-\bar x)^2}\ (\text{ddof}=0) $$
$$ \underbrace{\frac{\overline{e}}{\sigma_{\text{pop}}(e)}}_{\text{daily Sharpe}}\ \times\ \sqrt{P}\ =\ \text{annual Sharpe} $$

The code's _sharpe forms the excess series e = r − r_f/P first: the annual risk-free rate is de-annualized by simple division (not geometrically) so it lives on the same per-bar scale as the returns. Mean and dispersion are then computed on that same excess series.

The denominator is the population standard deviation, ddof=0 (divide by N), not the textbook sample ddof=1. Since bars are daily, P equals bars-per-year, and the periodicity annualization factor is exactly √P — read at call time from the mutable global _ACTIVE_PPY (365 for 24/7 crypto, 252 for stocks via the annualization() context manager).

The daily Sharpe ē / σ(e) is a dimensionless signal-to-noise ratio of one bar. Annualizing sums P i.i.d. bars: mean scales like P and std like √P, so their ratio scales like P/√P = √P. That √P blow-up is the entire difference between the daily and headline numbers — and why switching 252↔365 alone moves the reported Sharpe by √(365/252) ≈ 1.20× on identical returns.

Sortino: only the left tail is charged for risk

Same mean, same spread — but Sharpe divides by all volatility, Sortino divides by the RMS of the below-zero returns over all N.

$$ \text{DD} = \sqrt{\dfrac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^{N}\big(\min(e_i,0)\big)^2}\,,\qquad e_i = r_i - r_f/\text{ppy} $$
$$ \text{Sortino} = \sqrt{\text{ppy}}\;\dfrac{\overline{e}}{\text{DD}}\,,\qquad \text{Sharpe} = \sqrt{\text{ppy}}\;\dfrac{\overline{e}}{\sigma_{\text{pop}}(e)} $$

Sharpe treats every deviation from the mean as risk. That is symmetric: a windfall +8% day inflates the denominator exactly as much as a -8% crash. For an investor who only fears losses this over-penalizes upside surprises. Sortino fixes the denominator so only harmful volatility is charged.

The code (backtester.py:203-215) forms excess returns e = r − r_f/ppy, then neg = e.clip(upper=0) — every positive return is zeroed, every negative kept. Downside deviation is DD = sqrt(mean(neg²)). Two non-textbook choices are load-bearing: the mean divides by all N (not the count of losing days), and deviations are measured from a hard target of 0 (not from the subset mean). Both keep DD honestly small — the earlier subset-std form divided by fewer observations and re-centred on the loss mean, shrinking the denominator and inflating Sortino.

Numerator and annualization are identical to Sharpe: mean excess return, scaled by √ppy (365 crypto, 252 stocks). So Sortino/Sharpe = σ_pop(e)/DD ≥ 1 always, and the two diverge exactly as the left tail fattens: a strategy with clustered small losses and rare big gains keeps DD low while σ balloons, so Sortino ≫ Sharpe. This is why the FEP gate floors Sortino at 1.0 but Sharpe only at 0.7 (fep_robustness.py:33) — the same edge, read through a downside-only lens, must clear a higher bar.

The Omega ratio

Every gain above the line, weighted by how far — over every loss below it

$$ \Omega(\theta) = \dfrac{\sum_{r_i > \theta}(r_i - \theta)}{\sum_{r_i < \theta}(\theta - r_i)} $$
$$ \Omega(\theta) = \dfrac{\int_{\theta}^{\infty}\big(1-F(r)\big)\,dr}{\int_{-\infty}^{\theta}F(r)\,dr} $$
$$ \Omega(\mu)=1 \;\Longleftrightarrow\; \theta=\mathbb{E}[r]\;\;(\text{break-even}) $$

The discrete form the backtester ships (_omega, backtester.py:218) is a pure ratio of sums: numerator is the total distance of every return that lands strictly above the threshold θ, denominator the total distance of every return strictly below it. Ties (r == θ) are dropped from both — the code uses strict > and <. With no losses the denominator is 0 and the function returns +inf. The shipped threshold is θ = 0, not the risk-free rate, and nothing is annualized: Ω is dimensionless.

The integral identity is the reason Ω is more than a dressed-up win/loss count. Integrating the numerator sum by parts turns it into the area of the upper tail of the CDF, ∫(1−F) above θ; the denominator becomes the area of the lower tail, ∫F below θ. So Ω absorbs the entire shape of the distribution — every moment, skew and fat tails included — whereas Sharpe collapses it to just mean and variance.

The break-even fact is exact and worth internalizing. Numerator minus denominator equals Σ(rᵢ − θ) = N(μ − θ). That difference is zero precisely when θ = μ, and a ratio whose top and bottom are equal is 1. So Ω(θ) = 1 exactly at the mean: slide θ below the mean and Ω > 1, above it and Ω < 1. Drag the line onto the mean marker and watch the readout snap to 1.000.

In the FEP gate this is a green-vote metric with floor Ω ≥ 1.15 (COBRA_RATIO_FLOORS, fep_robustness.py:33) — measured at θ=0, so it asks whether upside mass beats downside mass by a clear 15% margin, not merely whether the strategy is net-positive.

Profit Factor

Gross dollars won per dollar lost — the 1.5 floor means 50% more won than lost.

$$ \text{PF} = \frac{\sum_{\text{pnl}_i > 0}\text{pnl}_i}{\left|\sum_{\text{pnl}_i < 0}\text{pnl}_i\right|} $$
$$ \text{PF} = \frac{W\cdot \overline{w}}{L\cdot \overline{\ell}} = \frac{p}{1-p}\cdot R,\quad R=\frac{\overline{w}}{\overline{\ell}},\ p=\text{win rate} $$
$$ \text{PF}\to+\infty \ \text{when } L=0;\qquad \text{floor: } \text{PF}\ge 1.5 $$

Profit Factor is gross dollars won divided by gross dollars lost. The code (backtester.py:392) computes it on the per-trade P&L table, not the return series: wins = pnl[pnl>0], losses = pnl[pnl<0], then PF = wins.sum() / abs(losses.sum()). Zero-P&L trades fall out of both sums; when there are no losers it returns np.inf.

The closed form makes the sliders meaningful. With N trades, win rate p and payoff ratio R = avg win / avg loss, the win count is W=pN and loss count L=(1-p)N. Fixing avg loss to 1 unit and avg win to R, gross won = pN·R and gross lost = (1-p)N·1, so PF = [p/(1-p)]·R. N cancels — PF depends only on the win/loss odds and the payoff ratio, never on how many trades you take.

This is why PF and win rate are near-independent knobs: a 40% win rate with R=3 gives PF = (0.4/0.6)·3 = 2.0, beating a 60%/R=1 coin-flip (PF = 1.5). The COBRA gate (fep_robustness.py:33) sets the edge floor at PF ≥ 1.5 — 50% more gross dollars won than lost — one of four per-metric floors a ratio TPI must clear (with Sortino ≥ 1.0, Omega ≥ 1.15, Sharpe ≥ 0.7).

The visual draws each simulated trade as a signed bar and accumulates the two gross totals as stacked columns; the readout is the exact ratio of those two column heights, so it always equals the formula above.

Annualization: time is a square root

Daily Sharpe scales by √ppy — and using crypto's 365 on a stock over-credits its edge by 1.2035×

$$ \text{ppy} = \begin{cases} 365 & \text{crypto (default)} \\ 252 & \text{stocks} \end{cases} $$
$$ \text{Sharpe}_{\text{ann}} = \sqrt{\text{ppy}}\;\frac{\overline{(r-r_f/\text{ppy})}}{\sigma_{\text{pop}}(r-r_f/\text{ppy})} = \sqrt{\text{ppy}}\;\cdot\;\text{Sharpe}_{\text{per-period}} $$
$$ \frac{\sqrt{365}}{\sqrt{252}} = \sqrt{\tfrac{365}{252}} = 1.2035\quad(\text{basis-mismatch inflation}) $$

Every annualized ratio in the backtester (_sharpe, _sortino, Annual Vol, Info Ratio) is a per-period statistic multiplied by a single scalar √ppy. The reason is variance additivity: for i.i.d. daily returns, mean scales linearly with the number of periods (k·μ) while standard deviation scales with √k (√k·σ). Their ratio therefore scales by k/√k = √k. With k = ppy daily bars per year, the annualization factor is exactly √ppy — not ppy.

The code reads ppy from a mutable module global _ACTIVE_PPY at call time: 365 for crypto (24/7 trading, _TRADING_DAYS) and 252 for stocks (_STOCK_TRADING_DAYS), swapped via the annualization() context manager. The mean excess uses de-annualized risk-free r_f/ppy and the denominator is the population std (ddof=0), so the whole ratio is a clean √ppy · (per-period Sharpe).

The trap: the factor differs by basis. √365 / √252 = √(365/252) = 1.2035. Annualizing a stock with the crypto basis of 365 multiplies its true (252-based) Sharpe and Sortino by 1.2035× — a ~20% free inflation. A stock whose honest annualized Sharpe is the FEP edge floor of 0.70 would read 0.70 × 1.2035 = 0.842 under 365, spuriously clearing the gate. That is why stock TPI scoring must enter annualization(252) before scoring — otherwise the COBRA floors are silently loosened for every non-crypto name.

The perturbation math — does it survive being poked?

Robustness = the edge persists when you perturb the strategy. Four perturbation axes, four estimators — the temporal (WFE), parameter (CoV), path (Monte-Carlo) and signal (coherence) tests.

Walk-Forward Efficiency (WFE)

The fraction of your in-sample edge that survives honest, out-of-sample re-optimization — trade-weighted across sliding folds.

$$ \rho_k = \begin{cases}\min\!\big(\tfrac{\text{tcore}_k}{\text{rcore}_k},\,3.0\big) & \text{rcore}_k > 0.10\\[2pt] 0 & \text{otherwise}\end{cases} $$
$$ \text{WFE} = \dfrac{\sum_{k=1}^{K} w_k\,\rho_k}{\sum_{k=1}^{K} w_k},\quad w_k = \text{Trades}^{\text{test}}_k \quad(\text{else } \tfrac1K\textstyle\sum_k\rho_k \text{ if } \sum w_k=0) $$
$$ T_{\text{wf}} = \sum_{k} \text{Trades}^{\text{test}}_k,\quad \text{certifiable} = [\,T_{\text{wf}} \ge 30\,] $$
$$ \text{wf\_fail} = [\,\text{WFE} < 0.50\,] \wedge [\,T_{\text{wf}} \ge 30\,] $$

What WFE measures. Each anchored, expanding fold trains on an in-sample slice and is judged on a held-out OOS tail. We score both windows with a trade-count-agnostic quality core core = (Sharpe/2 + Sortino/3 + Ω/1.35 + PF/4)/4, clamped ≥0. The per-fold retention ratio is ρk = tcorek/rcorek — how much of the training edge survived out-of-sample. ρ=1 means the edge fully generalized; ρ=0.5 means half of it was overfit noise.

Two deliberate non-textbook choices. (1) The ratio is capped at 3.0 before weighting, so one lucky fold can't dominate; and it's forced to 0 when the train core ≤ 0.10, killing spurious "greens" born from a near-dead training fold (dividing a tiny OOS number by a tiny IS number). (2) The aggregate is trade-weighted: wk = the fold's OOS trade count. A 2-trade fold carries almost no weight; a 40-trade fold anchors the estimate. If every fold records zero test trades the code falls back to a plain mean.

Why the ≥30 gate exists. WFE is a ratio of noisy estimates. Below 30 total OOS trades it is neither trustworthy enough to hard-fail a TPI (conditional WFE) nor to certify one (uncertified ⇒ ROBUST capped at BORDERLINE). This is exactly why a trade-starved book (median ~17 OOS trades) collapses to mostly BORDERLINE. The 0.50 floor only bites once the ≥30 certification threshold is crossed.

Coefficient of Variation — Plateau vs Spike

A robust edge doesn't care about the exact parameter; a curve-fit spike lives or dies on one integer.

$$ \text{per\_metric\_cv}[m] = \operatorname{round}\!\left(\frac{\sigma_{\text{pop}}\big(\{v \in V_m : v\ \text{finite}\}\big)}{\big|\,\mu(V_m)\,\big|},\ 4\right),\quad |\mu| > 10^{-9} $$
$$ V_m = \big\{\, f(p_0 + k) : k \in \{-3,-2,-1,1,2,3\} \,\big\},\qquad \sigma_{\text{pop}} = \sqrt{\tfrac{1}{N}\textstyle\sum (v-\mu)^2}\ \ (\text{ddof}=0) $$
$$ \text{cov\_stable} = \neg\big(\,\text{isnum}(\text{CoV}_{\text{worst}}) \wedge \text{CoV}_{\text{worst}} > \text{cov\_cap}\,\big),\qquad \text{cov\_cap} = 0.20 $$

Selection tunes a TPI to a single best parameter p₀. The question robustness asks is not "how good is the peak?" but "how much of the peak survives if I was one step off?" The estimator (tpi_autogen.py:1009-1016) sweeps one integer parameter over its ±3 neighborhood — k∈{-3,-2,-1,1,2,3}, the tuned point itself excluded — re-evaluates a COBRA metric at each, and reports the coefficient of variation of those six realizations.

CoV is scale-free dispersion: σ/|μ|. Two deliberate choices match the code. First, population standard deviation (ddof=0, numpy default) — this is a descriptive spread of the realized neighborhood, not an inference about a larger population, so the N-1 Bessel correction is wrong here. Second, the denominator is |μ| (absolute mean), so a negative-signed metric like drawdown cannot flip CoV negative and produce a nonsensical gate.

The gate (fep_robustness.py:122) is hard, not advisory despite the ADVISORY_COV_CAP name: if the worst metric's CoV strictly exceeds cov_cap (default 0.20), the TPI is stamped FRAGILE regardless of how strong its tuned edge looks. Note the strict >: at exactly 0.20 the hard gate passes while the read-only advisory (≥0.20) fires — a one-tick asymmetry that is intentional.

Geometrically: a plateau means f is nearly flat across the neighborhood, so σ→0 and CoV→0 — the edge is a property of the region, not the point. A spike means f(p₀) towers over its neighbors, so σ is large relative to |μ| and CoV blows past the cap — the "edge" is an artifact of landing on one exact integer, i.e. a curve fit.

Monte-Carlo Survival: does the edge outlive its own shuffle?

Resample the trades, redraw the equity curve a thousand times, and count how many still end in the black.

$$ \text{mc\_pos\_frac} = \frac{1}{N}\sum_{j=1}^{N}\mathbf{1}\!\left[\,E_j(T) > E_j(0)\,\right],\qquad E_j(k)=\prod_{t=1}^{k}\bigl(1+r^{(j)}_t\bigr) $$
$$ r^{(j)}\ \text{= a bootstrap resample of the realized per-trade returns } \{r_1,\dots,r_T\} $$
$$ \text{FRAGILE gate:}\quad \text{mc\_pos\_frac} < 0.50 \;\Rightarrow\; \text{fail}_{MC}=\text{True} $$

The live gate reads mc_pos < 0.50 → FRAGILE (fep_robustness.py:132). A companion fail-closed guard (tier-0, 2026-07): when the MC null has too few effective simulations to be trustworthy (mc_unreliable), the TPI is stamped FRAGILE rather than passing on an unreliable null — an untrustworthy test counts as a failed one, never a free pass. The pedagogical question it answers: is the equity curve an edge, or one lucky ordering of the same trades? If you keep the exact multiset of per-trade returns but reshuffle/resample the sequence, a genuine edge compounds positive almost regardless of order; a fluke needs its specific lucky path.

Here we draw \(N\) bootstrap resamples \(r^{(j)}\) of the realized trade returns, compound each into an equity curve \(E_j(k)=\prod_{t\le k}(1+r^{(j)}_t)\), and count the fraction whose final equity clears the \(E_j(0)=1\) start line. That fraction is mc_pos_frac — reported here exactly as \(\frac1N\sum_j \mathbf 1[E_j(T)>1]\). (The production mc_pos_frac in tpi_autogen.py:879 is the sibling significance form — the percentile of the real score inside an OHLC-permutation null — but both share the identical ≥0.50 survival floor and the same intuition: beat ≥half the reshuffles.)

Because bootstrapping preserves the return distribution and only scrambles order, mean drift dominates the survival count. Turn up the mean and the cloud lifts off the start line and mc_pos_frac→1; flatten the mean toward zero and it collapses toward the coin-flip 0.50 gate — the exact boundary at which the code stops trusting the path.

Coherence and the cube reward

A committee that doesn't agree with itself has nothing to say — and cubing makes that brutal.

$$ \text{coherence} = 0.4\,\overline{A} + 0.6\,\min_{i<j} A_{ij}, \qquad A_{ij} = \frac{1}{T}\sum_{t=1}^{T}\mathbf{1}\!\left[\tilde s_{i,t} = \tilde s_{j,t}\right] $$
$$ \overline{A} = \frac{2}{n(n-1)}\sum_{i<j} A_{ij}, \qquad \tilde s = \operatorname{nan\_to\_num}(s,\,0) $$
$$ \text{reward} = \big(\max(0.02,\ \text{coherence})\big)^{3} \qquad \text{(gate: FRAGILE if coherence} < 0.45) $$

A TPI is a committee of n child indicators, each emitting a sign per bar. Coherence asks how often two children agree in sign. For every pair (i,j) the agreement A_ij is the fraction of the T bars on which their (NaN-filled) raw signals are exactly equal — a bar-by-bar ==, not sign(), so in practice this counts matching quantized votes.

The scalar coherence blends two views: 40% the mean pairwise agreement (is the room broadly aligned?) and 60% the single worst pair (is any pair actively fighting?). The heavy weight on min A_ij is a deliberate destructive-interference guard — two children that cancel each other drag the whole score down even if everyone else nods along. With n<2 the score is defined as 1.

The Optuna objective then multiplies the risk/quality base by coherence³ (floored at 0.02 so a zero never annihilates the gradient). Cubing is the punishment: an incoherent committee at 0.5 keeps only 0.5³ = 0.125 of its edge, while a tight 0.9 keeps 0.729. Separately, fep_robustness hard-fails any TPI whose coherence falls below 0.45 — a noisy committee is FRAGILE regardless of its headline metrics.

The selection-bias math — why we do NOT chase it

The one piece of heavy machinery we tested and rejected. The math shows why best-of-N inflates a Sharpe even with zero edge — and why the deflation is unreliable on a ~6-trial, trade-starved book.

Deflated Sharpe: why best-of-N lies

Pick the luckiest of N zero-edge strategies and its Sharpe is inflated by chance alone — DSR subtracts that mirage.

$$ SR_0 \;=\; \sqrt{\operatorname{Var}(\hat{SR}_{\text{trials}})}\;\Big[(1-\gamma)\,Z^{-1}\!\big(1-\tfrac{1}{N}\big)+\gamma\,Z^{-1}\!\big(1-\tfrac{1}{Ne}\big)\Big],\quad \gamma=0.5772156649 $$
$$ \mathrm{PSR}(SR^*)=\Phi\!\left(\dfrac{(\hat{SR}-SR^*)\,\sqrt{n-1}}{\sqrt{\,1-\gamma_3\,\hat{SR}+\tfrac{\gamma_4-1}{4}\,\hat{SR}^2\,}}\right) $$
$$ \mathrm{DSR}=\mathrm{PSR}(SR_0)=\Phi\!\left(\dfrac{(\hat{SR}-SR_0)\,\sqrt{n-1}}{\sqrt{\,1-\gamma_3\,\hat{SR}+\tfrac{\gamma_4-1}{4}\,\hat{SR}^2\,}}\right) $$

The lie. Run N zero-edge strategies. Each estimated Sharpe SR̂ is a noisy draw around 0 with cross-trial spread Var(SR̂). The maximum of N such draws is not 0 — Gaussian extreme-value theory says the expected best-of-N sits at SR₀ above, growing like Z⁻¹(1−1/N). That is the deflation benchmark: the Sharpe the luckiest worthless strategy is expected to hit. The γ-blend of Z⁻¹(1−1/N) and Z⁻¹(1−1/Ne) is Bailey & López de Prado's closed-form for E[max]; γ is Euler–Mascheroni, and Var is the sample variance (ddof=1) of the per-period trial Sharpes.

The correction. PSR is a t-test on the Sharpe: Φ of the standardized gap (SR̂−SR*)√(n−1), with the denominator widening for negative skew γ₃ and fat tails γ₄ (non-excess kurtosis). PSR(0) asks "is the edge positive at all?"; DSR just raises the bar from 0 to SR₀. Since SR₀≥0, DSR ≤ PSR always. Sharpe here is per-period (daily) — no √252 inside.

Why it was reverted here. The generator's effective trial count is the number of distinct combos that competed — under greedy beam search, N≈6. With so few trials Var(SR̂) is unestimable, SR₀ collapses toward 0, and DSR degenerates to plain PSR(0) (flagged 0.561 vs control 0.568 — indistinguishable). A pre-split PSR pair-filter even made the rotation worse (OOS Sharpe 1.04→0.34). All three prototypes were reverted; only the genuine forward-decay PSR stays live. Slide N below ~10 to watch SR₀ and the deflation vanish.

The low-N evidence rescue (rule R1, 2026-07-14) — starvation is physics, not guilt

At our long-only cadence (~7 direction changes a year) a pair simply cannot generate many trades — roughly 5 out-of-sample trades is normal. Treating "too few trades" as an automatic FRAGILE would punish tempo, not quality. So a starved pair (below the window-scaled minimum trade count) is rescued to BORDERLINE, badged "low N" (small sample), when — and only when — every other measurable axis is strong. The rescue is evaluated only after every non-starvation hard gate (Monte-Carlo shuffle, parameter-CoV, time-consistency, coherence, turnover, cross-asset) has passed, and then requires all four of:

  • the worst row of the parameter sweep still clears ≥ 3 of 4 edge floors (sweep-worst edge ≥ 3/4);
  • the out-of-sample window clears ≥ 3 of 4 edge floors — the load-bearing safeguard (it independently blocks the SOLDOGE / SUIDOGE / SUIXRP 0-of-4 cases regardless of Monte-Carlo noise at 20 simulations);
  • Monte-Carlo path survival ≥ LOW_N_MC_MIN = 0.85 — stricter inside the rescue than the ordinary 0.50 gate;
  • parameter-dispersion stable and time-consistency stable (the time condition is structurally guaranteed at this point — a tripped time gate would have landed in step 0 — but it is stated in the code on purpose: a starved pair that is measurable and era-scattered must never be rescued).

The outcome is BORDERLINE, never ROBUST — the evidence is thin, not bad. Failing the rescue appends "too few trades" and the verdict is FRAGILE as before. Simulated against the operator's 55-pane quality review (2026-07-14): 12 rescues, 0 operator-rated-BAD pairs upgraded.

5Walk-forward efficiency — does the edge survive honest re-tuning, and can we even measure that?

Walk-forward efficiency (WFE) answers one everyday question: if we periodically re-tune the committee on everything seen so far and then trade it forward on data it has never seen, does the edge survive the re-tuning? (Pardo 2008's walk-forward efficiency: the mean ratio of out-of-sample to in-sample performance across re-optimization folds.) A reading at or above WFE_STABLE_FLOOR = 0.50 means the strategy keeps at least half of its tuned strength when it is honestly re-fit and traded forward — the temporal-stability bar that ROBUST requires. The interactive fold widget in the mathematics part (Walk-forward (WFE) lab) lets you drag the fold count, edge decay and trade counts and watch this number move.

Anchored, expanding folds — always judged on the held-out tail

The folds are anchored and expanding (_fold_windows in tpi_autogen.py): every fold trains on everything from the start of history up to the fold, then tests on the fold window itself, so later folds always know more — exactly like the real workflow of periodically re-tuning a live committee. Three honesty details are built in. First, the test folds are anchored to begin at the in-sample end (the walk-forward call passes initial_frac = is_frac), so the generalization verdict is judged strictly on the tail that the combo-selection search never saw. Second, a 5-bar embargo is cut from the end of each training window so an indicator's trailing lookback cannot straddle the train/test seam. Third, nothing vanishes silently: folds removed because the tail was too short (each test window needs at least ~30 bars; the fold count shrinks until that holds) are counted and reported (n_folds_requested / n_folds_skipped_short / n_folds_failed), so the report can honestly say "3 of 4 folds".

The per-fold ratio, and the trade-weighted mean

Each fold's generalization ratio compares the core performance measured on the held-out test window against the same measure on its training window, with two safety rails taken straight from the code: the ratio is capped at 3.0 so one lucky fold cannot dominate the mean, and a fold whose training core is negligible (below 0.10) scores 0 rather than exploding toward infinity:

$$ r_k \;=\; \begin{cases} \min\!\left(\dfrac{\text{test core}_k}{\text{train core}_k},\; 3.0\right) & \text{train core}_k > 0.10 \\[6pt] 0 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases} \qquad\quad \text{WFE} \;=\; \frac{\sum_k w_k\, r_k}{\sum_k w_k},\quad w_k = \text{test trades of fold } k $$

The mean is trade-weighted: a fold that actually traded 12 times says more about generalization than a fold that traded twice, so it counts proportionally more. (A 2026-07-14 bug fix matters here: a fold whose trade count came back as NaN — not-a-number — used to silently disable the weighting for exactly the most starved pairs; _wf_fold_trades now treats NaN as zero.)

The fold-validity guard — a one-trade fold is a coin flip (2026-07-14)

At the slow long-only cadence our book actually trades (roughly 7 direction changes a year), a 4-month test fold holds about one trade — and then its "generalization ratio" is just the sign of that one trade, a coin flip dressed up as a measurement. The book audit that motivated the guard found 84% of folds held two or fewer trades, and six zero-trade folds scored a perfect capped 3.0. So two guards now apply (constants in fep_robustness.py):

  • A fold with fewer than WF_FOLD_MIN_TRADES = 3 test trades does not count. It is non-informative and is excluded from every walk-forward aggregate (mean ratio, positive-fold fraction, recent ratio).
  • Fewer than WF_MIN_VALID_FOLDS = 2 informative folds means there is no measurement. Walk-forward efficiency is then reported as "not measurable (n/a)" (wfe_status = "na_too_few_events") instead of a number that would read as pass or failure but is really noise.

The certifiable regime — with enough trades, the number always exists and the floor is live

When the folds together hold at least WFE_MIN_TRADES = 30 total out-of-sample (OOS — data the tuning never saw) trades, walk-forward efficiency is certifiable: the aggregate uses the trade-weighted mean over all folds, so "not measurable" is unreachable by construction, and the WFE_STABLE_FLOOR = 0.50 hard FRAGILE gate is fully live. Below 30 total trades the number (when it exists) is advisory: it can no longer hard-fail the committee, but it also cannot certify temporal stability, so a would-be ROBUST verdict is capped at BORDERLINE (wf_uncertified).

The monotonicity invariant (adversarial review, 2026-07-14): "not measurable" must never earn a better verdict than any measured number could. Two mechanisms enforce it: (1) every n/a state — guard-tripped, young, or no folds at all — caps a would-be ROBUST at BORDERLINE exactly like the uncertified case; (2) in the certifiable regime n/a is unreachable, so the hard-fail path cannot be dodged by arranging for thin folds. In fep_verdict the n/a check is keyed on the ratio being absent or the explicit status — never on whether the fold list is empty — so the young path cannot slip past the cap either.

Young assets are tagged, not scored

Under WF_YOUNG_MIN_YEARS = 2.5 years of history, not even one informative fold is expected (36-day folds on the HYPE pairs put the expected trade count near 0.3): walk-forward is structurally impossible there, not merely noisy. Such assets are tagged "young" — the folds are kept for display, the aggregates are None — and the pane shows a tag instead of a punishing red 0.0. The BORDERLINE cap above still applies, so youth is never an advantage.

Cadence-aware fold sizing — make the folds fit the tempo (2026-07-14)

A slow committee cannot be measured with fast folds, so the fold design adapts to the committee's tempo:

  • Slow committees get two long folds. If the committee trades fewer than SLOW_TRADES_YR_THRESHOLD = 12.0 trades per year on the in-sample (IS — the tuning window) span, the walk-forward runs 2 long folds of roughly 12 months instead of the default 4 short ones (n_folds_eff = 2 in _compute_report), so each fold holds enough trades to clear the 3-trade validity guard at all. The trades-per-year rate is measured on metrics_is, never on the full period — using the full period would leak the walk-forward tail into the fold design.
  • Slow long-only ratio runs start the tail earlier. For long-only ratio-basket generation (LONG_ONLY_SCORING with ratio_pairs) the default in-sample fraction drops from the historical 0.75 to SLOW_WF_IS_FRAC = 0.65 (config.py), leaving a walk-forward tail of about 2.1 years on a ~6-year pair — exactly enough room for the two ~12-month folds to exist. An explicitly passed fraction always wins.

6Parameter CoV — why spread is a gate

Around the tuned point we sweep each parameter ±3 and recompute the metrics. The coefficient of variation (std/mean) of the worst metric measures how much the edge scatters when you nudge the knobs. A tight neighbourhood (low CoV) means a plateau — the edge doesn't depend on hitting an exact parameter. A wide one means a spike you probably curve-fit. CoV > the cap (default 0.20) is a hard FRAGILE gate; the Auto-TPI pane can tighten the cap per run, and — as of the June-2026 reconciliation — that slider reaches the live verdict, not just the display.

6bTime-consistency — the TIME neighbourhood

Does the committee make its money in every stretch of history, or in one lucky era? The TIME neighbourhood is the temporal twin of the ±3 parameter sweep: instead of nudging the knobs, we nudge the era — and demand the scorecard barely move.

Why a second temporal gate — the walk-forward starves, daily returns do not

The load-bearing temporal check used to be walk-forward efficiency (WFE) — re-optimize on each fold, test on the held-out tail, require the edge to keep ≥ 50% of its strength. WFE is trade-based, and that is its physical limit on this book: a slow long-only committee decides roughly seven times a year, so a 4-month test fold holds about one trade, and its "generalization ratio" is the sign of a coin flip. The 2026-07-14 book audit made this concrete: 84% of walk-forward folds held ≤ 2 trades, and six zero-trade folds scored a perfect 3.0 (fep_robustness.py, the PAXGDOGE lesson). The conditional-WFE rule (§5) already refuses to let that noise hard-fail anyone — WFE may only veto when the folds together hold ≥ WFE_MIN_TRADES = 30 out-of-sample (OOS) trades — but the same rule means that on a trade-starved book the temporal axis was mostly unmeasured, not merely noisy.

Daily-return metrics do not starve. A quarter of our pre-lockbox history is a 14–18-month slice holding ~300–450 daily returns — plenty for Sharpe, Sortino and Omega, which are computed from every bar rather than from trade events. So the TIME neighbourhood scores each slice on exactly those three (_TIME_COV_METRICS = ("Sharpe", "Sortino", "Omega")); Profit Factor, %Profitable and trade counts at 3–8 trades per slice are order-statistic noise and are recorded but never gate. Division of labour, stated once: TIME = does the locked committee's edge exist in every era (always measurable past 2.5 years of history); WFE = does the edge survive re-optimization (certifiable only at ≥ 30 OOS trades). ROBUST requires both.

Companion repair on the WFE side (2026-07-14): when the in-sample window shows fewer than SLOW_TRADES_YR_THRESHOLD = 12 trades per year, the walk-forward runs 2 long (~12-month) folds instead of 4 short ones, so each fold holds enough trades to measure anything at all; slow long-only ratio runs also default to a 0.65 in-sample fraction (config.SLOW_WF_IS_FRAC) so the walk-forward tail is ~2 years. The trades/yr trigger is read from the in-sample metrics only — never the full period, which would leak the walk-forward tail into the fold design.

Construction — four slices, scored exactly as production trades

  • Span. The pre-lockbox history [START_DATE, lockbox start). At the generation call site the frame is already lockbox-truncated and end_ts=None, so the slices are structurally incapable of touching the reserved 6-month holdout (§14a) — leak-free by construction, not by promise.
  • Cut. TIME_K_WINDOWS = 4 equal contiguous slices — quarters of a ~5–6.5-year span, i.e. ~14–18 months each, regime-sized. Halves, thirds and rolling windows were tested during calibration and rejected: no separating power, or an inverted GOOD/BAD ordering.
  • Score. The locked committee (tuned parameters frozen — this is a stability probe, not a re-fit) is scored per slice on the production profit-and-loss basis: long-only traded sign (a bearish read rotates to cash, never short — _traded_sign), net of the run's transaction cost (_active_cost_bps), and on the correct annualization basis (365 bars/yr crypto, 252 stocks). The composite signal is computed once; only K sliced backtests follow — the cheapest robustness axis in the report.
  • Validity. A slice shorter than TIME_WIN_MIN_BARS = 60 bars is dropped; fewer than TIME_MIN_VALID_WINDOWS = 4 valid slices ⇒ the axis is "n/a (too few history slices)". A history shorter than WF_YOUNG_MIN_YEARS = 2.5 years ⇒ "young". Both are handled below — non-blocking, but never free.

Gate 1 — the spread across eras (TIME-CoV), and why it fails closed

For each daily-return metric we ask how much its per-slice values scatter, using the coefficient of variation (CoV) — the standard deviation divided by the absolute mean — and then judge the worst of the three metrics:

$$ \mathrm{CoV}_{\text{time}}(m) \;=\; \frac{\sigma_{\text{pop}}\!\big(m_1,\dots,m_K\big)}{\lvert\,\overline{m}\,\rvert}\,, \qquad m \in \{\text{Sharpe},\ \text{Sortino},\ \Omega\},\ \ K = 4 $$
$$ \text{TIME-CoV}_{\text{worst}} \;=\; \max_{m}\ \mathrm{CoV}_{\text{time}}(m) \;\le\; \texttt{TIME\_COV\_CAP} = 1.10 \qquad \text{(hard FRAGILE gate)} $$
$$ \mathrm{CoV}_{\text{time}}(m) \;=\; +\infty \quad \text{when } \lvert\overline{m}\rvert \le 10^{-9} \ \text{ or fewer than 2 finite slices} \qquad \text{(fail closed)} $$

The fail-closed rule is deliberate and is the one place this gate differs philosophically from its parameter twin. The parameter-sweep CoV (§6, neighborhood_robustness) writes NaN when a metric's neighbourhood mean is degenerate — it fails open, because the parameter axis is backstopped by six other hard gates. The TIME axis is often the only temporal gate a trade-starved pane will ever face (WFE being advisory there), so an unmeasurable spread must read as "not stable" (inf, a hard fail with the plain-language reason "unstable across time"), never as a silent pass.

Gate 2 — no dead stretch of history (the strict worst-slice floor)

Spread alone is not enough: a committee can post a tight CoV around a mediocre mean while one entire era earned nothing. So the worst slice's core performance — the same trade-count-agnostic blend the walk-forward uses (_core_perf, roughly 1.0 when a slice's metrics sit at the §3 edge floors, the “COBRA green” levels) — must stay above a floor:

$$ \text{core}_k \;=\; \max\!\Big(0,\ \tfrac{1}{4}\Big(\tfrac{\text{Sharpe}_k}{2} + \tfrac{\text{Sortino}_k}{3} + \tfrac{\Omega_k}{1.35} + \tfrac{\min(\text{PF}_k,\,10)}{4}\Big)\Big) $$
$$ \min_{k}\ \text{core}_k \;\ge\; \texttt{TIME\_CORE\_FLOOR} = 0.05 \qquad \text{(STRICT minimum — hard FRAGILE gate)} $$

This is a strict minimum on purpose. The obvious softening — drop the single worst slice, mirroring the walk-forward's outlier-tolerant worst fold — was prototyped during calibration and rejected: it passed every single operator-rated-BAD pane. The dead slice is not an outlier to forgive; it is the one-lucky-era signal the gate exists to catch. The floor's value is not arbitrary either: 0.05 sits just below the weakest slice of any rated-GOOD pane in the calibration set (ETHSUI's quiet quarter, core ≈ 0.06), so honest lulls pass while a genuinely dead era cannot. (Outside the set the gate is stricter than the operator's eye: four GOOD-rated panes fail it — see below.)

Young assets and unmeasurable panes — n/a is non-blocking but never free

A pair younger than 2.5 years, or one with fewer than 4 valid slices, gets time_status = "young" / "na_too_few_windows" and None values: the axis does not gate (you cannot fail a measurement that does not exist). But the same monotonicity invariant that governs the WFE-n/a case applies: an unmeasured pane must never out-rank a measured one. A would-be-ROBUST verdict with TIME n/a is capped at BORDERLINE (time_na_capped, reason "time-consistency n/a … stable but temporally unverifiable"), exactly parallel to the WFE-n/a cap of §5.

7Annualization — same floors, correct scale

Sharpe and Sortino are annualized by \(\sqrt{\text{periods/yr}}\). Crypto trades 24/7 (365 bars/yr); equities trade ~252 sessions. Using 365 for a stock inflates its Sharpe/Sortino by \(\sqrt{365/252}\approx1.20\times\) against floors calibrated on the crypto book — a spurious credit for a whole asset class. The June-2026 fix makes annualization asset-class-aware (backtester.annualization(), set per run by the autogen wrapper). Profit Factor and Omega are scale-invariant, so only Sharpe/Sortino move.

Re-scoring the 16 stock TPIs on the 252 basis produced zero verdict flips — the correction never crosses a floor decisively (it can only lower Sharpe/Sortino, so it can only ever demote, and none were that close). It is a units-correctness fix, not a re-labelling. The basis is now passed as an explicit periods_per_year at every stock scoring site (backtest, OOS, dashboard, forward test), with a thread-local ambient fallback for the generation pipeline — so a background stock-generation job can never poison metrics computed concurrently on a dashboard request thread (the old process-global _ACTIVE_PPY could).

Transaction costs — the floors are gross; costs live at the portfolio layer

Be explicit about what the edge floors do not include: the per-TPI COBRA metrics (Sharpe 0.70, Sortino 1.0, PF 1.5, Omega 1.15) are computed on a frictionless equity curve. The TPI scoring path (signal_to_equity → Backtester.from_price_series) has no cost hook, so every verdict floor is gross-of-cost by construction. That is deliberate — a per-pair TPI is a vote, not a tradable book — but it must be stated, not assumed.

Costs are modeled one level up, at the rotation / portfolio layer, by a toggleable venue-aware fee model (config.FEES_ENABLED, default off; Backtester.net_portfolio_returns is the single accounting core shared by the headline equity and both OOS evaluators, so they can never diverge net-vs-gross). Buys pay buy_bps, sells pay sell_bps — Korean/Taiwanese sell-side transaction taxes live on the sell leg — plus per-class slippage; cash legs are never charged, so an asset→asset switch books sell+buy while an asset→cash flip books one side. 9The two-regime candidate search & the coherence reward

A naïve top-k beam builds redundant committees — five flavours of the same moving average — because coherence rewards indicators that agree, and same-family indicators trivially agree. So the search runs two regimes and merges them:

Diverse regime

A family taxonomy (MA, momentum, band, oscillator…) with a MAX_PER_FAMILY = 2 cap. Forces the committee to span mechanisms, so agreement means something.

Free regime

No family cap — lets a genuinely strong same-family stack win when it deserves to. Merged via weak-dominance top-k so the free beam is never silently evicted by the diverse one.

The score keeps a coherence reward, score = base · coherence³. We tested dropping it and rejected that: without it the committee's signal degrades into noise (an incoherent committee is just a random ensemble). Diversity is enforced by the family cap, not by punishing coherence — the two are orthogonal, and conflating them was the original redundancy bug.

The search tunes parameters to maximize an in-sample hybrid score, then robustness is checked as a post-hoc filter. It's natural to ask: shouldn't the search itself seek robustness? We prototyped exactly that, and the honest answer is no — at the per-pair layer, robustness effort doesn't pay:

Pipeline updates (2026-07-14/15) — the beam repair, incumbent seeding, the time_robust ledger stage, and honest refusal

Four changes to the search machinery of §8–§11, shipped with the time-consistency gate (§6b). Each exists because a hard gate somewhere else created a blind spot the search could fall into.

The beam repair — a hard wall at the beam made the ranking meaningless; now a steep discount

The beam stage ranks indicator sets cheaply, by compositing each child's solo-tuned parameters — no joint tune has happened yet. That shortcut has a systematic bias: solo-tuned children run roughly 2× whippier than the same set tuned jointly, because the solo screen applies no tempo pressure (nothing rewards a child for agreeing with committee-mates it has not met). When the time-coherence cadence gate was applied as a hard zero at this stage, it judged every candidate on behaviour joint tuning would later fix — and on ETHBTC it zeroed ~99% of beam evaluations. Ranking over a field of zeros is meaningless, and it had a concrete casualty: the live incumbent's PSAR+ST seed pair scored 0 at beam, so the known-good committee was unreachable by construction, and the shipped replacement was worse on every recorded metric.

The repair (2026-07-14): at the beam only, a cadence-violating set is scored at a multiplicative ×0.30 discount instead of zero (ledger tag cadence_beam_soft_* — penalized, not rejected). Clean sets still dominate the ranking, but a set that joint tuning could calm stays comparable instead of invisible. Nothing degenerate can ship through this softening, because the gate stays hard everywhere that matters: inside the tuning objective (an Optuna trial whose tuned signal violates cadence scores 0.0, ledger cadence_tune_*) and at final combo-level selection (a violating tuned combo is refused outright — see the refusal semantics below).

Why the soft/hard split is safe: the beam only decides which sets get a joint tune; the tune and final gates decide what can ship. A discount at the proposal stage cannot leak a whippy committee into the book — it can only stop the proposal stage from starving the tune of its best candidates.

Incumbent seeding — a regeneration may only replace the live committee by beating it under the same judges

The same ETHBTC episode exposed a second blind spot: the live committee is a known-good candidate the beam can no longer see (its seed pair dies under solo-tuned params), so a regeneration could silently replace a healthy incumbent with something worse simply because the incumbent never entered the race. Now it always does:

  • Seeded into the joint tune. The current on-disk committee (the codes of robustness_<name>.json) is appended to the combo list, tagged "incumbent" — provided it matches the run's size, has no duplicate or dead-on-tuning children, and every code is in the active pool. Best-effort and never fatal: a malformed artifact skips the seeding, it cannot crash a run.
  • Snapshot source. The environment variable RSPS_INCUMBENT_DIR redirects the seed to a snapshot of a known-good book (e.g. master's live committees) instead of the working tree — because the on-disk book may itself be a degraded regeneration output (the very thing being replaced), and re-seeding a degraded incumbent recovers nothing.
  • Always judged. After tuning, the incumbent joins the full-robustness-report set unconditionally — additively, so the weak-dominance top-k of §9 (the exact eval set the old free-only beam would have produced) is untouched. It then competes under the identical selection key: verdict rank, then the robust-or-stable conjunction (_stable: walk-forward floor, parameter-CoV cap, time-consistency), then the performance rank (_perf_rank).

The consequence is a one-way ratchet: a regeneration can only replace the incumbent with a candidate that honestly out-ranks it under the same judges. If everything the search finds is worse, the incumbent wins its own regen; if everything is refused by the hard gates, the incumbent stays by refusal (below). Either way "regenerate" can no longer mean "quietly downgrade".

The time_robust ledger stage — new evaluations, still an honest N

The time-consistency check (§6b) adds K sliced backtests per candidate. Every one of them is counted in the trial ledger (§14b) under its own stage, time_robust — same pattern as the mc, sweep and walk_forward stages. Like those, it is a diagnostic stage: the evaluations are recorded for audit but excluded from selection_trials_n, the honest N behind the expected-max-of-noise null — a slice backtest diagnoses an already-chosen combo, it is not a draw against the null. So the new gate makes the search more auditable without inflating (or deflating) the selection-over-noise benchmark.

Honest refusal — refuse, descend, keep the incumbent, flag

Three hard walls stand at final selection, in order: the cadence gates (whippy or tempo-incoherent tuned combos), the parameter-CoV reject (worst-metric coefficient of variation (CoV) over the cap, default 0.20 — the MUDELTA 112% lesson), and the time-consistency reject (spread over 1.10 across history slices, or a dead slice — §6b). Each discards individual candidates first (ledger tags cov_final, time_cov_final); the interesting case is when a wall eliminates every candidate. The pipeline then refuses to ship the least-bad. Instead, _descend_on_refusal:

  • Descend. Drop one child and rerun the whole search at the smaller size (5 → 4 → 3, down to min_size = 3 — two-voter committees cannot ship). The per-run caps (max_metric_cv, max_time_cv) are forwarded through both recursion sites, so a descent can never silently loosen a gate. The old code only descended on a FRAGILE verdict; a size-5 whose candidates were all hard-rejected simply gave up, even when a clean smaller committee existed (SOLPAXG: no acceptable 5-child set, but a clean 3-child NKAMA+DUAL+AAMDMOM passes everything).
  • Refuse. If even min_size yields nothing acceptable, the run raises "no acceptable committee; keeping the incumbent TPI" — a first-class outcome, not an error to retry. Nothing is written; the live book keeps the incumbent.
  • Flag. The refused pair is recorded in RSPS/incoherent_pairs.json with its refusal reason. The dashboard pane carries a loud plain-language badge (red "NOT REBUILT — OLD SIGNAL KEPT", or dark "VOTE MUTED" when the sleeve's mute policy is on — §12b), so a refusal is impossible to mistake for a certification.
The philosophy, stated once: shipping an unacceptable committee is worse than failing the generation. A refusal keeps a known quantity voting (or, under §12b, silences it measurably for free) and tells the operator the truth — that no time-coherent, parameter-stable, era-consistent committee currently exists on that cross at this search budget.

12The ceiling is the asset, not the search

Put §10 together and a clear picture emerges: the per-pair robustness layer is at the asset ceiling. On a book with a median of 17 OOS trades, the honest state of the world is that most TPIs cap at BORDERLINE — not because the search is weak, but because there isn't enough out-of-sample evidence to certify a temporal edge. That is a feature: the label tells the truth about what we can and cannot verify. The equity is untouched by all of this — the rotation votes every signal ungated; the verdict is an honest label, never a capital gate.

12bWhen no trustworthy committee exists — the vote-mute policy

A refused pair (§11b) leaves the rotation with a question: should the stale, uncertified incumbent keep voting with real money? The answer is measured, per sleeve, and it differs — which is exactly why it had to be measured.

What a muted vote is — a tie, not a hole

A pair for which the latest regeneration could build no gate-passing committee is listed in RSPS/incoherent_pairs.json. Under the mute policy (2026-07-15) such a pair casts a zero vote in the round-robin tournament: its strength series is returned zeroed — same index, every value 0 — so the matchup counts as a tie and the ranking is decided by the provable matchups. Nothing is dropped or re-plumbed: the tournament simply learns nothing from that cross.

  • One chokepoint per sleeve. Crypto: RSPS_main.pair_strength_series_engine consults muted_pairs() (gated by config.MUTE_REFUSED_VOTES). Stocks: RSPS_stocks.stock_pair_strength_series (gated by config.MUTE_REFUSED_VOTES_STOCKS). Because the zeroing happens where the series is produced, the backtest, the live weights and the dashboard display all see one consistent muted signal — no path can disagree.
  • Fails open, deliberately. A missing or unreadable incoherent_pairs.json yields an empty refused set: a broken file must never silently mute the whole book. (The list is mtime-cached and re-read on change.)
  • Reversible per pair. A future regeneration that passes the gates removes the pair from the refused list and the vote comes back automatically — the mute is a statement about the current evidence, not a blacklist.

12cDerived votes through the PAXG hub — vote algebra for the muted matchups

The mute policy (§12b) is honest but leaves a hole: with 10 of the 36 crypto matchups declared ties, the tournament ranks the assets on less information. This section describes how a muted matchup gets its ranking vote back without building any new strategy — by deriving it, with plain algebra, from two committees that already exist and already passed every gate.

The idea in one line

A ratio of ratios cancels: ETH/BTC = (ETH/PAXG) ÷ (BTC/PAXG). So if the system cannot build a trustworthy committee directly on ETH/BTC (its only profitable era in the sample was 2021 — a one-era edge every gate correctly refuses), it can still answer the only question the tournament actually asks — "is ETH stronger than BTC right now?" — by comparing two signals it does trust: ETH-versus-gold and BTC-versus-gold. Both are certified-stable committees (§4 tiers). The derived vote is simply

derived vote(A/B)  =  ½ · ( strength(A vs PAXG) − strength(B vs PAXG) ),   clipped to [−1, +1]

Orientation is corrected automatically (a PAXG/BTC committee speaks about gold-vs-bitcoin; its sign is flipped to read bitcoin-vs-gold). Both legs must be active, certified-stable committees: a muted hub leg can never power a derived vote — in that case the matchup stays a tie.

Why this adds no curve-fitting risk

  • Zero new parameters. Nothing is tuned, searched, or selected: the derived vote is a fixed function of two existing signals. There is no way for it to "overfit" — it has no degrees of freedom of its own.
  • Stability is inherited, not claimed. Every input is a committee that passed the full ladder (time-coherence, the six stability axes, the tier gates). A difference of two stable signals moves as slowly as its parents.
  • Complete coverage by construction. Every crypto major has an active, certified-stable PAXG pair in the book — gold is the natural hub because gold-crosses are the most stable committees we have (7 of the 8 PAXG pairs are STABLE-ROBUST). The stocks analogue (MSTR hub) exists but is rejected — see the measurement.
  • Declared, not hidden. A pair voting through the hub carries a visible "◈ VOTE DERIVED — via the PAXG hub" badge on its pane, and the whole mechanism is a labelled switch on the Overview panel (crypto ON / stocks permanently OFF), persisted with Save Settings, so the active policy is always one glance away.

14Clean evidence — the lockbox, the trial ledger, and what a verdict may claim

The 2026-07-08 assessment's P2 finding was blunt: every performance number the system produced was, to an unknown degree, a selection statistic. The labelled "OOS" window overlapped the TPIs' own training spans, candidate selection touched held-out data in three places, and the search evaluated thousands of parameter configurations per TPI with no record of how many things were tried. Classic ML pipelines solve this with a test set nothing may touch and an experiment log; the RSPS analogues are the lockbox holdout and the trial ledger (assessment fixes 1.2 / 1.3, shipped 2026-07-11). Both are report-only by design: the moment either fed a gate or a selection key it would become one more thing the optimizer overfits — Goodhart's law is the failure mode this section exists to prevent.

14a · The lockbox holdout — data the pipeline cannot see

At generation time the final LOCKBOX_MONTHS = 6 of history are cut off at the data-fetch boundary (tpi_autogen._lockbox_holdout, truncation inside _fetch_basket). Because the truncation happens before anything else runs, every downstream stage is structurally blind to that window — the solo screen, the beam search, Optuna tuning, the candidate ranking, the flat-child filter, the walk-forward folds, and the final pick cannot read it even by accident. This is stronger than a promise not to look; the data simply is not there.

After the winner is chosen — and only then — the pipeline evaluates it exactly once on the reserved window and writes a write-once holdout block into the robustness JSON: the edge floors cleared on genuinely unseen data, the trade count, and the regime context. That number is the only per-TPI statistic in the system that is free of selection bias at birth.

  • What it never touches: the FEP verdict, _stable, _perf_rank, ship_ok, the selection sort. A TPI cannot be chosen because its lockbox looks good — that would silently convert the test set into a validation set.
  • Exemptions: assets with < LOCKBOX_MIN_HISTORY_YEARS = 2 of history, and mixed-maturity baskets whose shortest member is too young (truncating would starve tuning). LOCKBOX_MONTHS = 0 disables the mechanism.
  • Cost: newly generated TPIs tune on data ending ~6 months ago. That is the price of owning a test set.
  • Honest limitation: at ~12–20 trades/yr, a 6-month lockbox holds only a handful of trades — it can screen a TPI for gross overfit at birth, but it cannot certify an edge by itself. Certification power lives at the portfolio level and in the forward channels below.

14b · The trial ledger — counting what the search tried

The false-strategy theorem (§M3) says the two sufficient statistics of a backtest audit are how many things were tried and how much their Sharpes varied — try enough noise and the best draw looks like an edge. The generator now records exactly that: a thread-local ledger counts every stage of the search and is snapshotted into each new robustness JSON as _trial_ledger:

  • Provenance: seed, budget, sizes_tried, combos_tuned, per-stage optuna_trials (screen / tune / walk-forward) and raw evaluations counts.
  • selection_trials_n — the honest N: every parameter configuration the search drew while hunting the winner (solo screens + combo tuning + beam combo scores). Walk-forward re-optimizations and MC/sweep evaluations are recorded but excluded from N — they refit or diagnose an already-chosen combo, they are not draws against the null.
  • expected_max_sr_null — the Bailey–López de Prado expected best in-sample Sharpe of selection_trials_n zero-skill trials, using the empirical cross-trial Sharpe dispersion (sr_trials.std) actually observed during the search — not a distributional assumption. sr_excess_over_null is the headline: the tuned in-sample Sharpe minus that null. Positive by a clear margin ⇒ the edge exceeds what selection over noise explains; ≤ 0 ⇒ the number is indistinguishable from picking the best of N noise draws.
  • sric — the Paulsen–Söhl Sharpe Information Criterion (k tuned parameters, T years): the within-trial optimization-bias bound, complementing the across-trial null above. Neither alone captures total overfit; both point the same direction.
  • Report-only, enforced: nothing reads the ledger back — no verdict, gate, selection key or ship decision consumes it (tpi_autogen.py ledger block). Thread-local so dashboard display evaluations never contaminate a generation's counts.
Why the retro-fitted numbers were only estimates: the pre-ledger book (everything generated before 2026-07-11) has no recorded N or cross-trial dispersion, so the Phase-A probe (reports/TPI_HONESTY_2026-07-11.html) had to bracket N at 30–120 and assume a √(P/T) null dispersion. Its conclusion — 52/55 trade-starved, 31–46/55 within selection-over-noise at the honest budget — is the case for regenerating under the ledger, where these numbers become exact instead of estimated.

12dWhen does a pair vote? — the conditions, in plain terms

The rotation is a round-robin: every pair of assets is one match (9 crypto majors → 36 matches), and the pair's committee decides who wins it. Win more matches, get more weight. So "does this pair vote?" is the same question as "is this match played?".

The four conditions

  1. A committee exists for that pair — it was generated and is in the shipped book.
  2. The pair is not muted — the latest regeneration managed to build a committee that clears the gates. If it refused (no candidate passed stability / time-coherence, even at the smallest committee size), the pair is recorded in incoherent_pairs.json and its vote is neutralized.
  3. Both legs have data at that moment — HYPE simply did not exist before November 2024, so every HYPE match before then is unplayed, not lost.
  4. The signal is usable — not a missing value. Since the 2026-07 audit this fails closed and visibly; before, a missing value silently became a phantom win for whichever asset was listed first.

A match not played is NOT a draw

This distinction is load-bearing and was fixed in the audit. An unplayed match awards no points and also leaves the denominator: each asset is ranked on its win rate over the matches it actually played, never on raw win counts. Otherwise an asset that plays 8 matches and wins 4 would out-rank one that plays 3 and wins all 3 — rewarding volume instead of skill. An asset that played no match cannot be ranked at all, which is also why a not-yet-listed asset can never be handed the "worst asset" slot in the 80‑20 mode: a position in something that does not exist yet is not a position.

What happens to a muted vote, per sleeve

Crypto — the vote is not simply dropped: it is derived through the PAXG hub (§12c), rebuilt with plain algebra from the two legs' certified-stable gold committees. Only if a hub leg is unusable does the match go unplayed. Stocks — the incumbent keeps voting, flagged red on the dashboard, because muting was measured harmful there: the stock tournament is sparse and winner-takes-all, so every vote is load-bearing (Sharpe 2.02 → 1.91 when muted). Both directions were measured; neither was chosen by taste.

Why any of this exists. A committee that failed the gates has no demonstrated edge. Letting it vote because "something must be said" is precisely how an unstable signal moves real money. Today 17 of 55 pairs are in that state — and the portfolio decomposition (§17) shows the rotation still earns in all four eras, because the tournament is redundant and the mutes are honest.

After the July audit the operator asked the right question: if so many pairs refuse, are the constraints simply too strict — and can we tune the search to produce more stable, more robust committees? That question was tested to destruction over four experiments. The answer is unambiguous, and it saves anyone from re-running the search-tuning program in future.

What it means

Robustness lives in the pair, not in the search. Gold-versus-meme holds up under every setting; alt-versus-alt and alt-versus-BTC hold up under none. The optimizer is already well-calibrated — every variation either matched the baseline or made things worse — and no re-weighting manufactures an edge that the ratio does not have. Two practical consequences:

  • Cadence earns its cost. The search spends 99.9% of its rejections satisfying cadence, which looked wasteful. Removing it halves the yield: without it the search wanders into whippy regions that then fail the time and significance gates. It is not overhead, it is what keeps the search in a sane neighbourhood.
  • Never steer toward a gate. Certification must rest on evidence the objective cannot see — the untouched lockbox and the luck benchmark (§14). Anything the search can optimize, it will, and then the gate stops being evidence.

The acceptance posture that follows

Stated by the operator and now the written policy: first look for a solution that is robust and time-coherent; if none exists, take one that is stable and time-coherent, maximizing robustness within that. The order is safe because it is nested — robust is a subset of stable, which is a subset of time-coherent — so preferring the robust one never trades away stability. And "look for robust" must always mean search broadly and certify honestly, never steer the objective at the robustness metric (E7).

16b · The two jobs the word "robustness" does — and which one D16 tested

Read this before quoting D16. "Robustness" covers two different jobs in this system. Mixing them up turns a narrow, useful result into a false alarm about the whole assessment method. It is the single easiest misreading to make here, so it is written down.

Job A — the admission examJob B — ranking among the admitted
The question Is this committee trustworthy at all? Among committees that already passed A, do the higher-tier ones win more matches?
What it decides Ship it, or refuse it and mute the vote Whether to weight votes by tier inside the tournament
What it compares passed the gates vs failed them ROBUST vs EDGE vs THIN — all of which already passed
Status Validated, unchanged, not on trial in D16 Tested in D16 — no signal on that window

D16 never pitted an admitted committee against a refused one. Every committee in every scenario had already cleared the admission exam. STABLE-THIN does not mean "bad" — it means "passed, with a thinner measured edge than STABLE-ROBUST". So the comparison was "very good versus good enough", never "good versus bad".

What follows, concretely:

  • The pipeline is unchanged. Assess every TPI → deploy only what passes → mute or derive the rest. Job A is why 17 of 55 pairs are refused today, and nothing in D16 challenges it.
  • One idea is parked — the one we had not built yet: giving higher-tier committees more voting weight than other already-admitted ones. On the tested window there is no evidence to build that from. Re-decided by the forward re-test at +3 / +6 months.

Why the two jobs can disagree without either being wrong. The exam asks whether a method held up across history, parameter jitter and different eras. A tournament match is decided by whether that committee happens to be right on that particular day. A well-vetted method and a lucky day are different things — nothing forces them to line up, and on this window they did not. That is a statement about the limits of what a historical exam can predict about tomorrow's single call, not a verdict on the exam.

The portfolio's real safety net is structural, not per-committee. 36 redundant matches, refused votes muted or re-derived through the gold hub, nine assets, two sleeves. That is why the rotation earns in all four eras (§17) while 17 of its members individually fail the era test. The system was built so that no single committee's verdict is load-bearing — which is also why tier gradations among the admitted have little left to move.

OQOpen questions — what is NOT settled

The affirmed decisions of §13 record what we committed to. This section records the opposite, with the same discipline: the places where the current design is known to be provisional, fitted, or unproven. Nothing here is hidden in a footnote on purpose.

OPEN · 2026-07 · theoretical — VERIFIED not occurring (2026-07-15)

The dead-slice floor could punish a macro gate for doing its job — checked: it has not.

The time-consistency gate demands that a strategy make money in every stretch of history (no dead slice: the worst of the 4 history slices must keep a core score ≥ 0.05). For a pair committee that is exactly right — a dead quarter means one lucky era. In principle a macro gate (QQQ / TOTALES / OTHERS.D / PAXG) — a whole-sleeve risk switch whose correct behaviour in a bear market is to sit in cash and earn nothing — could be mislabelled "dead" for a correctly-flat bear slice. Checked against the data (2026-07-15): this has not actually happened. The QQQ demotion at the 3bcacaa relabel (worst-slice core 0.001) was deserved, not a mislabel: the old committee traded 9 times through the 2021–23 bear slice and lost money there (slice Sharpe −0.74) — it was churning long into the decline, not sitting out. The v5 regeneration, forced by this gate, replaced it with a committee that crosses the same bear slice roughly flat-positive (slice Sharpe +0.14, worst-slice core 0.31, BORDERLINE) — the gate worked as intended. All four macro gates pass the time gate in the current book. A macro-gate exemption (judging risk-off slices on avoided drawdown rather than earned return) therefore stays a designed-for edge case only: revisit if a future regeneration punishes a gate that was genuinely flat-and-right in a falling market.

OPEN · 2026-07

The time-consistency thresholds are fitted numbers, not derived ones.

The two constants of the time gate — spread across history slices (worst-metric coefficient of variation, CoV) capped at 1.10, and the dead-slice floor at 0.05 — were calibrated against the operator's pane review (52 rated panes: 20 GOOD, 6 BAD, 26 in between), fitted on a 12-pane subset (all 6 BAD + 6 GOOD) with no held-out ratings. The calibration is comfortable rather than knife-edged (the worst GOOD pane in the set sits at 0.984 and the best BAD pane at 1.241, with SOLPAXG at 1.006 cleared by design, and BAD detection is insensitive to the cap anywhere in 1.00–1.25) — but a threshold fitted to 12 labels with no holdout is a fitted threshold. Treat it exactly like a tuned parameter: it must be re-derived at each regeneration epoch against fresh operator ratings, never carried forward as if it were a law of nature (fep_robustness.py: "Recalibrate at each regen epoch").

OPEN · 2026-07

Certification still targets components, not the thing that holds money.

Every ROBUST / BORDERLINE / FRAGILE stamp certifies one pair committee as a standalone strategy. The portfolio — the rotation that actually holds capital — has measurement channels (the held-out window after the split (rotation out-of-sample, OOS), the untouched final-6-months lockbox, and the forward-decay monitor with its ~50 one-way trades per year of statistical power, §14c) but no verdict of its own: there is no gate that says "this rotation, as configured, is certified" and no formal composition rule from pair labels to a portfolio label. Portfolio-level certification is the roadmap; until it exists, the honest summary is that we certify the bricks and monitor the house.

OPEN · 2026-07

The new book's forward superiority over the live book is UNPROVEN.

The v5 book is the first selected under every honesty gate, and that is a reason to prefer it on principle — not proof it will earn more. On the same backtest window (out-of-sample 2024→2026, net of fees) the v5 crypto rotation scores a Sharpe of 1.38 against the live book's 1.82, and the stock sleeve 2.02 against v4's 2.33 (8733723). Re-running that comparison until a favourite emerges would be selection over books — the exact sin the lockbox exists to prevent, and the regen commit warns against it explicitly. The only evidence that can settle "is the honest book also the better book?" is the forward monitor on data that did not exist at generation time. Until that evidence accumulates, the claim stays unproven and this document says so.
